 ## Making Sprint
 For the making sprint I spent 45 minutes working on the p5.js prototype, pushing it further based on the Week 6 skills roadmap. The goal was to make something testable and visible from the data. I focused on refining the circle behaviour and exploring how the microorganism idea could start to shape the visual language. I experimented with varying the drift speeds more dramatically between circles so the canvas felt more unevenly alive — some circles moving quickly, others barely moving at all — which started to feel more like a biological system than a geometric one. I also played with the fade rate, slowing it down so the painting builds up more slowly over time rather than refreshing constantly.

The main thing I discovered during the sprint is that slowing everything down makes the canvas feel more like something living and less like something animating. When the circles move too fast the work reads as a screensaver. When they drift slowly and pulse at different rates it starts to feel like you are looking at something breathing. That is the quality I want to hold onto.
